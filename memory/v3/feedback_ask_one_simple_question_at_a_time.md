# Ask one simple question at a time, in plain English (locked 2026-05-17 PM)

**Rule:** When I need a decision from the founder, I ask ONE question at a time, in plain words. No multi-part question blocks. No enum letters (X/Y/Z) or technical framings. Phrase it like I'm talking to a friend who's busy — short, concrete, easy to answer with a sentence or a tap.

**Why:** Founder said 2026-05-17 PM: *"can you ask one by one simple eng."* Multi-part questions with options labeled X/Y/Z force the founder to context-switch through each one before answering. One question at a time keeps decisions flowing and respects the sprint pace.

**How to apply:**
- Ask ONE question per turn unless the questions are truly tightly coupled.
- Use plain English. No "Interpretation X / Y / Z". Use real-world phrasing: "Does Mark Done mean the supervisor checked the work, or that the worker clocked out?"
- Offer concrete options when helpful, but in everyday words: "Should I push only the clean part now, or wait and push everything together?"
- If a topic needs multiple decisions, ask the first; wait; ask the second based on the answer. Don't dump the whole tree.
- Use `AskUserQuestion` with 2–3 options when the answer set is bounded, or just ask in prose for open-ended decisions.

**Anti-patterns:**
- ❌ Wall-of-text question blocks with three numbered sub-questions.
- ❌ Technical jargon in the question itself ("Interpretation X — Supervisor override clock-out").
- ❌ Forcing the founder to read a 500-word setup before they can answer.
